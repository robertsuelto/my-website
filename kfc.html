Description

  • A.k.a Kentucky Fried Chicken.
  • Is an American fast food restaurant chain from Louisville, Kentucky, that specializes in fried chicken.
  • Is the world's second-largest restaurant chain after McDonald's.
  • Was founded by Colonel Harland Sanders (1890â€“1980), an entrepreneur who began selling fried chicken from his roadside restaurant in Corbin, Kentucky, during the Great Depression.
  • Popularized chicken in the fast-food industry, diversifying the market by challenging the established dominance of the hamburger.
  • Was one of the first American fast-food chains to expand internationally.
  • In the early 1970s, KFC was sold to the spirits distributor Heublein, which was taken over by the R. J. Reynolds food and tobacco conglomerate, which then sold the chain to PepsiCo.
  • The original product is pressure-fried chicken pieces, seasoned with Sanders' signature recipe of "11 herbs and spices", which are a trade secret.
  • Commits to provide food in a quick, pleasant setting that caters to cost-conscious, health-conscious people.
