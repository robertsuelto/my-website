Description

  • Is an American multinational fast food chain founded in 1940 by Richard and Maurice McDonald, in San Bernardino, California, United States. They rechristened their business as a hamburger stand, and later turned the company into a franchise, with the Golden Arches logo being introduced in 1953 at a location in Phoenix, Arizona.
  • In 1955, Ray Kroc, a businessman, joined the company as a franchise agent and proceeded to purchase the chain from the McDonald brothers. McDonald's had its previous headquarters in Oak Brook, Illinois, but moved its global headquarters to Chicago in June 2018.
  • Is the world's largest fast food restaurant chain, serving over 69 million customers daily in more than 100 countries in over 40,000 outlets as of 2021.
  • Is the world's second-largest private employer with 1.7 million employees.
  • There are over 36,000 McDonald's restaurants worldwide. Over a third of these are in the United States alone. The second highest number is in Japan with 2,975 restaurants, followed by China with 2,700 branches. These three countries make up the majority of global McDonald's stores.
  • Serves a locally relevant menu of food and beverages.
